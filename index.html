The Boy Who Lived

On his eleventh birthday, Harry Potter discovers he is no ordinary boy — he is a wizard. Thus begins an extraordinary journey across eight cinematic tales of friendship, courage, love, and the eternal battle between light and darkness. From the marble corridors of Hogwarts to the shadowy depths of the Forbidden Forest, this chronicle recounts the complete story of the boy who changed the wizarding world forever.

Film One

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone Gryffindor

Released 2001 · Directed by Chris Columbus
⚡

The journey begins on a humble London street, where Harry Potter — an orphaned boy living under the stairs — receives a letter that will change his life forever. From the towering Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, Harry finds a world where staircases move, paintings talk, and every subject is a spell away from wonder. He makes his first true friends, Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, and together they uncover a mystery that stretches back to the Dark Lord himself.

At the heart of the castle lies a powerful secret — the Philosopher's Stone, an ancient artifact capable of granting immortality. Harry, Ron, and Hermione venture through deadly obstacles guarded by a three-headed dog named Fluffy, withstand a life-sized game of wizard's chess, and face their deepest fears in the Forbidden Forest. In the climax, Harry confronts the shade of Lord Voldemort himself, learning the truth of his scar and the prophecy that binds his fate.

"It does not do to dwell on dreams and forget to live." — Albus Dumbledore
Film Two

Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets Slytherin

Released 2002 · Directed by Chris Columbus
🐍

Dobby's warning echoes too late: the Chamber of Secrets has been reopened. A mysterious heir of Salazar Slytherin stalks the halls of Hogwarts, petrifying Muggle-born students and unleashing a basilisk deep beneath the school. Harry discovers he can speak Parseltongue, the language of snakes — a gift tied to the Dark Lord's power.

Guided by a ghostly diary that turns out to be a Horcrux — a vessel for a fragment of Voldemort's soul — Harry descends into the chamber itself. There, beside the weeping statue of Slytherin, he faces not only the serpent but the memory of a young Tom Riddle, whose ambition and cruelty were forged at Hogwarts. With the help of Fawkes the phoenix and the legendary Sword of Gryffindor, Harry destroys the diary and saves Ginny Weasley, unknowingly destroying a piece of Voldemort's soul.

"It is our choices that show what we truly are, far more than our abilities." — Albus Dumbledore
Film Three

Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban Ravenclaw

Released 2004 · Directed by Alfonso Cuarón
🌙

The tone shifts darkly as Sirius Black, a dangerous prisoner, escapes from Azkaban — the most guarded wizard prison — apparently to find and kill Harry. The Dementors, soul-sucking guards of Azkaban, patrol Hogwarts, forcing Harry to learn the complex Patronus Charm under the watchful eye of Professor Lupin — a man whose kindness conceals a lycanthropic secret.

Through Hermione's Time-Turner, the tale weaves a tapestry of time and sacrifice. The truth unfolds: Peter Pettigrew, disguised as Scabbers the rat, betrayed Harry's parents. Sirius Black is innocent. With the help of a werewolf, a dog Animagus, and a twist of time, Harry prevents an innocent man from receiving the Kiss of Death and gains a godfather he has always longed for, even as loss lingers in the cold air of Hogsmeade.

"Happiness can be found even in the darkest of times, if one only remembers to turn on the light." — Albus Dumbledore
Film Four

Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire Gryffindor

Released 2005 · Directed by Mike Newell
🔥

The Triwizard Tournament — a legendary contest between three schools of magic — returns to Hogwarts after a century of dormancy. Against a host of age restrictions, Harry's name springs from the Goblet of Fire, binding him to a deadly competition that sees him face a Hungarian Horntail dragon, merpeople beneath the lake's surface, and a labyrinth filled with both wonder and mortal peril.

The final task ends in tragedy. The trophy becomes a Portkey, transporting Harry and Cedric Diggory to the graveyard of Little Hangleton. There, under a blood-red sky, Lord Voldemort returns fully to life — his body restored, his Death Eaters rising behind him. Cedric dies. Harry returns with a body in his arms and a world on the brink. The war has truly begun.

"If you want to know what a man's like, take a good look at how he treats his inferiors, not his equals." — Sirius Black
Film Five

Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix Gryffindor

Released 2007 · Directed by David Yates
🦅

The Ministry of Magic refuses to believe Voldemort has returned, branding Harry a liar and a lunatic. Meanwhile, Dolores Umbridge arrives at Hogwarts as the new Defense Against the Dark Arts professor, enforcing a regime of silence and blood-quill punishment. Desperate to learn how to defend themselves, Harry, Hermione, and Ron form Dumbledore's Army — a secret student group preparing for the war the Ministry refuses to acknowledge.

At the Ministry's Hall of Prophecy, a ferocious battle erupts. Sirius Black falls through the veil in the Department of Mysteries, lost to Harry forever. Dumbledore duels Voldemort in the atrium, aurors arrive, and the truth is laid bare. Warborn hope returns, but grief lingers. The Order of the Phoenix stands ready. The war has no time for childhood.

"We've all got both light and dark inside us. What matters is the part we choose to act on." — Sirius Black
Film Six

Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince Slytherin

Released 2009 · Directed by David Yates
🧪

Darkness descends upon both wizarding and Muggle worlds. Death Eaters thrive, and Lord Voldemort's power grows unchecked. In a haze of fear and loss, Harry finds an old textbook in Potions class — annotated by the Half-Blood Prince, with secrets that make him the class's brightest star and lay the groundwork for a deadly curse.

Dumbledore reveals the nature of Horcruxes: fragments of Voldemort's soul hidden across the world. Together they journey to a cursed cave, where Dumbledore drinks a poison that weakens him irreversibly. At Hogwarts, beneath the Astronomy Tower, Severus Snape raises his wand. The curse strikes. Dumbledore falls. The Half-Blood Prince's identity is revealed. The hunt for Horcruxes begins — and Harry has no master now.

"Do not pity the dead, Harry. Pity the living, and, above all, those who live without love." — Albus Dumbledore
Film Seven

Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part I Ravenclaw

Released 2010 · Directed by David Yates
💀

The war reaches its climax. The Deathly Hallows — three legendary artifacts: the Elder Wand, the Resurrection Stone, and the Cloak of Invisibility — call to Harry. Abandoning the safety of school, Harry, Ron, and Hermione leave on a desperate mission to find and destroy Voldemort's remaining Horcruxes, hunted relentlessly by Snatchers and Death Eaters.

At Godric's Hollow, Harry visits his parents' graves and the ruined home where he was born. In the forest of Malfoy Manor, they are captured and tortured. Dobby arrives to free them — and falls. His final breath is a hero's end. The trio flees to Shell Cottage, where grief and determination sharpen their resolve. They retrieve the golden locket, unaware that its destruction requires a sacrifice far greater than magic. The road is long. The end is near.

"It is a strange thing, but when you are dreading something, and would give anything to slow down time, it has a curious way of speeding up." — J.K. Rowling
Film Eight

Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part II Gryffindor

Released 2011 · Directed by David Yates
⚔️

THE FINAL BATTLE BEGINS. Hogwarts stands as the last fortress of light. Voldemort and his army of Death Eaters and dark creatures converge on the castle. In this final chapter, every spell, every life, every friendship is forged in fire.

Harry walks into the Forbidden Forest, ready to die — and discovers that the Elder Wand's true master is Draco Malfoy, disarmed by Dumbledore on the tower. The Hallows are not the prize; love is. In the Great Hall, the final duel erupts. Expelliarmus against Avada Kedavra. When Voldemort's curse rebounds upon himself, the Dark Lord falls — not with a spell, but with the weight of his own choices.

Nineteen years later, the children of Harry and Ginny board the Hogwarts Express. An era ends. Another begins. The scar, at last, has not hurt for nineteen years. The story closes — but in every reader and viewer, it lives on.

"After all this time?" "Always." — Severus Snape & Albus Dumbledore
